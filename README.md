# 📊 Population/Public Health Research Project

## 🦠 Research Topic
Burden and Outcomes of Dengue-Related Hospitalizations in the United States

---

## 📌 Overview
This project investigates the epidemiology, clinical outcomes, and healthcare burden of dengue-related hospitalizations in the United States using secondary inpatient data.

Dengue is a rapidly expanding global public health concern, with approximately 390 million infections annually, of which about 96 million are clinically apparent. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

---

## 🎯 Objective
- Analyze dengue-related hospitalizations in the U.S.
- Identify high-risk populations
- Evaluate healthcare utilization (ICU admission, length of stay, mortality)
- Inform public health preparedness strategies

---

## 🧪 Methodology
- Retrospective observational study
- Data source: HCUP Nationwide Inpatient Sample
- Identified dengue cases using ICD-10 codes
- Applied statistical analysis and regression modeling

---

## 📈 Key Findings
- Dengue poses a growing burden due to climate change and travel
- Vulnerable populations (elderly, comorbid patients) have worse outcomes
- U.S. data on dengue hospitalizations remains limited

---

## ⚠️ Challenges
- Underreporting and misdiagnosis
- Limited U.S.-specific datasets
- Data coding inaccuracies
- Environmental unpredictability affecting transmission patterns

---

## 🔬 Future Directions
- Machine learning for outbreak prediction
- Improved surveillance systems
- Climate-based modeling
- Expanded U.S.-focused research
